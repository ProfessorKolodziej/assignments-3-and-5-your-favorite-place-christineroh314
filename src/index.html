Seoul

Seoul will offer eclectic, dramatically diverse experiences and a startlingly unique culture. It is a city with rich history and tradition, with centuries-old palaces, temples, and historic neighborhoods offering a glimpse into Korea's past. At the same time, Seoul is a vibrant and modern metropolis known for its futuristic architecture, bustling streets, and advanced technology. As a global hub for technology and innovation, visitors have the chance to experience cutting-edge gadgets, futuristic attractions, and immersive digital experiences.

While Seoul is a bustling urban center, it is also surrounded by natural beauty, with scenic parks, mountains, and the Han River providing opportunities for outdoor recreation and relaxation. Korean cuisine is renowned worldwide for its bold flavors and diverse dishes. Seoul offers a wide range of culinary experiences, from traditional Korean barbecue and kimchi to trendy cafes and international cuisine. Come to Korea, and you can have it all in one trip: art, history, food, tradition, coast, mountains, and fun.

  1. Changdeokgung Palace and Secret Garden

    Explore Changdeokgung Palace and Secret Garden

    While Gyeongbokgung is considered Korea's main palace, Changdeokgung and its secret garden actually served as the primary royal residence from the early 1600s to the 1800s.

    What makes Changdeokgung a unique palace to visit is its design. Instead of making the surrounding nature work with its layout, architects designed the palace to work in harmony with nature. When they worked on the Secret Garden, they kept artificial landscapes completely minimal to better highlight its natural setting. The Secret Garden was used for everything from banquets to recreation and even some farming throughout the centuries.

    It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, one of the most significant Korean landmarks, and the best preserved of all the palaces.

    Travel Tips

    • Book up to 6 days ahead of time.
    • While you can stand in line and get a ticket to see the palace grounds, you actually need to be on a specific tour to visit Huwon (the secret garden). If it's busy, the tickets actually sell out quickly.
    • Book “Seoul by Foot” (Secret Garden + Bukchon Hanok Village Walking Tour). You will join one of the 90-minute guided tours for Huwon, followed by a 2 1/2-hour walking tour that includes the rest of Changdeokgung, Bukchon Hanok Village, and Samcheongdong.
    • There are English, Chinese, and Japanese tours.
    • You can get there by taxi, the Seoul Bus, the local bus, or the subway. The subway would be the fastest and most reliable way to get there.
    • By subway would be the fastest and most reliable way to get there.
    • Address: 99, Yulgok-ro, Jongno-gu, Seoul

    History

    Changdeokgung was created in 1405 as the secondary royal palace. While Kim Taejong claimed it was because Gyeongbokgung's topography wasn't auspicious enough, many believe it's because he assassinated his brothers in the main palace and didn't want to rule from the same area. After the Japanese invasions destroyed all the palaces in the late 1500s, it was the first palace rebuilt and became the primary royal residence for almost 300 years.

  3. Bukhansan National Park

    The Ultimate Guide to Conquering the Bukhansan Hike in Seoul

    70% of Korea is covered with mountains and hills, and Seoul is no exception. Bukhansan National Park is located within the northern boundaries of Korea's capital and, as a result, is very easily accessible from anywhere in the Seoul Metropolitan Area. This makes it understandably popular, and Bukhansan is even in the Guinness Book of Records as the most visited national park per unit area, with around five million annual visitors.

    Bukhansan Mountain is also known as Samgaksan, which means “Mountain of Three Horns.” It has 81 trails for hiking and walking.

    Travel Tips

    • It is free!
    • All park entrances are easily accessible by public transport from anywhere within the Seoul Metropolitan Area, taking 20 minutes to 1 hour from Myeongdong or Seoul Station.
    • There are multiple peaks and hiking trails with easy-to-expert levels.
    • Baegundae Peak, the highest peak, is one of the most popular hiking trails. It starts easy with lots of steps and gradually gets steeper as you get closer to the peak. The final stretch is tough with rocky terrain.
    • More Info: Explore Bukhansan National Park

    History

    During the Joseon Dynasty (1392-1897), Bukhansan National Park was designated as a royal hunting ground for the nobility. This area served as a retreat for the royal family, who constructed many pavilions and temples. The Royal Family initially constructed the Bukhansanseong Fortress to defend Seoul from attacks, but the 120-room residence became a royal retreat.

  4. Insadong

    Explore Insadong

    Known as the cultural heart of the city, Insadong offers a unique blend of traditional and modern attractions, being near must-see spots like Gyeongbokgung Palace and Bukchon Hanoi Village. The main street is lined with traditional tea houses, art galleries, and antique shops. The area is also famous for its vibrant street food scene, offering a wide variety of local delicacies.

    Travel Tips

    • The Beautiful Tea Museum is free and opens year round. It is more of a cafe and less of a museum as the name would suggest, has a tea shop when you first enter and then tables that welcome guests to stop and try teas from the numerous collection. There are also porcelain works on display and for purchase from talented ceramists. At the tea shop inside the museum, over 110 kinds of tea from around the world including Korea, China, Japan, and Europe are on offer.
    • Consider Seoul Morning E-Bike Tour. A professional guide will guide you in both Korean and English for the new and diverse experiences that only electric bicycle travel can offer.
    • Other nearby attractions worth exploring include the Jogyesa Temple and the Bukchon Hanok Village, where you can immerse yourself in Korean culture and history.
    • You can drive past and learn about the President's home, Blue home.
    • Watch the changing of the guards and tour the spectacular Gyeongbokgung.
    • Visit the National Folk Museum if you're interested in learning Korean history.

    Trivia

    During the Joseon Dynasty, the buildings in the area were owned by merchants, bureaucrats, and government officials. Insadong is The best place to find calligraphy materials, folk crafts and souvenirs, and Hanji (traditional paper).

For their collaboration with Jennie, the brand released “Jentle Garden,” a mobile game where users could grow their flower garden and enter to win exclusive frames from the collection.
